# The-enteric-nervous-system-symbiont
Describing a symbiont living in the host with only the enteric nervous system as its form of communication. A thought experiment that maybe can help shed some light on the interaction of gastro-intestinal parasites on the psyche of their hosts.

## Enteric system symbiont

A body shared through the connection that is the Enteric nervous system, means the mood is shared between host and symbiont. Tapping into that mood means altering or disturbing the synchronization between host and symbiont. Ideally they should be identical, at least in their reception.

Every party that taps into the enteric nervous system from that perspective is disturbing the balance between generation and consumption of moods. An ideal symbiont produces what the host consumes and consumes what the host produces, benefiting a homeostatic equilibrium that turns the body into a state of rest, at least when omitting the transitions of states and focussing only on the steady-state responses of the control system made up of host and symbiont.

This means that the symbiont is an opposing force to the natural presence of the host, at least in the realm of the enteric nervous system. Attempting to pair physical vectors for these interactions, considering their electrical influences on the enteric nervous system would provide something like this:  If the host is feeling agitated expressed as inflammation (resentful + outraged), the symbiont will eat into that agitation/inflammation and produce guilt expressed as the resolution phase of the immune system (loyalty + hysteria).
The host is attacking a pathogen, the symbiont is repairing damage and suppressing the host’s actions. In doing so the actions of neither host nor symbiont are annulled but stabilized instead. The reactions, from a control theory perspective, are slowed down to a steady response with minimal oscillations.

## The conciousness and development of the symbiont
A normal symbiont, parasite or other relationship form or contract between host and resident, usually has the resident, symbiont in this case, reflect what the host is doing back at the same host. In that light, the resident is nothing more than a mirror to the concious efforts of the host to control the immune system.

Depending on the way this mirror is shaped, the resident is deemed a parasite or symbiont or other. 
- If the mirror is amplifying the immune reactions of the host, as is the case with some viruses, the resident is a kamikaze agent with an objective of complete destruction and annihilation. It tries to trigger cascading immune responses to overwhelm the defenses of the host, taking it out with it's own weapons. The goal of this resident, usually, is reproduction through destruction.
- If the mirror is reflecting the immune reactions of the host, as is the case with parasites, the resident is a dormant dweller that tries to feed in silence at the expense of the host. Ideally it would annul the immune system in the region that it is located in, practically it makes a compromise with the host to make a comfortable status-quo for both parties. If one can get the upperhand on the other, they will do so if it benefits them.

A symbiont from that perspective is a parasite that was pushed to the extreme through circumstance, so it had to start pulling it's own weight. It either had to abandon it's host or try to live on it's own, if the host ever died. But what if the host came close to dying, just enough of a primer for the parasite to start panicking and think of an exit strategy. If it wanted to abandon, it would need a mechanism that was designed and built well in advance. That is, atleast, what a prepared parasite would do. A dine and dash startegy, if you will.

However, an unprepared parasite will have to conform to the state of the host until it ceases. That will happen when the host eventually dies or sooner. Never later, it is unprepared for any such event. In that light, the parasite made a pact or intent to die with the host when it does. That solidarity of fate, intertwines their existences to compel both to invest in one another. The parasite will invest in the host if it needs to, ideally only when the situation is dire. From that point on, the host becomes a piggy bank to the parasite. The parasite takes resources out when times are good and makes a deposit when times are tough.

Then there's specialization, instead of taking and dumping the same resources over and over again. And in the process have the parasite starve itself to keep the host alive. Why not take something analogues that has less impact on the host? That way you can take more from the host and give less in return. As long as the parasite can take more, it will keep on specializing. Once it starts taking something that doesn't bother the host anymore, it becomes commensalism. Once it develops even further, and the host starts eating a resource that was bothering the host, it becomes a full on symbiont. The final stage of the unprepared and cowardly parasite, an unremovable part of a bigger organism. It's burning desire fulfilled, it will never have to look for a home again. And since it already made the vow to die with the host when it does, it gotten everything it has always wanted without realizing it.
